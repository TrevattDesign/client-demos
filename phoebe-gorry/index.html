About Phoebe

Trained in the body as well as the mind

Before I became a psychotherapist, I spent over 15 years working as a performer. That time shaped how I understand people: the way a body carries what a mind struggles to articulate, the particular pressures of creative and freelance life, and what it actually takes to keep showing up.

I hold a Master's in Counselling and Psychotherapy from the University of Roehampton and am a Registered Member of the BACP. I trained in somatic trauma therapy with world-renowned specialist Babette Rothschild, and that body-aware, trauma-informed way of working runs through everything I do.

I work in private practice in Surbiton and London, and as a sessional therapist at Nightingale Hospital. I've previously worked in NHS settings, with the low-cost service Headstrong, and with young creatives at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts. I offer an explicitly GSRD-affirmative space for LGBTQIA+ clients and those exploring gender and sexuality.

My approach

I don't follow a single method. I draw from person-centred, psychodynamic, somatic, and creative approaches and adapt to what each person actually needs. The common thread is attention to the whole of you, not just the story your mind is telling. Therapy can be short-term and focused, or longer and more exploratory. We work that out together.

How I work

The methods behind the method

01

Somatic and body-aware

Trauma lives in the nervous system as much as in memory. Drawing on my training with Babette Rothschild, I pay attention to what the body holds alongside what words can reach. This approach is particularly effective for trauma, anxiety, and chronic stress.

02

Person-centred at the core

You are the expert on your own experience. My job is to offer a consistently warm, non-judgmental space in which you can begin to hear yourself more clearly, and to trust what you find there.

03

Psychodynamic depth

Old patterns often have old roots. Where it's useful, we look at how early relationships and experiences continue to shape how you move through the world today, not to assign blame, but to understand.

04

Creative and imaginal

I use imagery, metaphor, and embodied awareness to open up what words alone can't always reach. Drawing on my background in performance, I understand how the creative and the psychological are often inseparable. This approach is never obligatory, always led by you.

05

GSRD-affirmative

I offer a fully affirming space for LGBTQIA+ clients, people exploring gender and sexuality, and those whose identities sit outside mainstream frameworks. You will not have to explain yourself, justify your experience, or manage my reactions.

What people often want to know

Before any paid session, we'll have a free 30-minute video call to talk about what's brought you here and see whether working together feels right. If it does, the first full session is a chance to go a little deeper at whatever pace feels comfortable. There's no pressure to cover everything at once, and nothing you say will be judged or rushed.

Talk therapy works primarily through language and reflection. Somatic therapy also pays attention to what's happening in the body: tension, breath, sensation, posture. This matters because trauma, anxiety, and distress are stored in the nervous system, not just in memory. Working somatically means we can reach things that words alone sometimes can't. It doesn't mean anything physical or intrusive, just a more body-aware conversation.

GSRD stands for Gender, Sexuality, and Relationship Diversities. Being GSRD-affirmative means I actively support clients who are LGBTQIA+, non-binary, polyamorous, kinky, or whose identity or relationships fall outside mainstream norms. You won't need to explain yourself or justify your experience. I bring knowledge, not just goodwill, and I understand the specific psychological pressures that come with minority stress and identity.
