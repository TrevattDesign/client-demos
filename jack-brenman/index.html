About Jack

Depth, rigour and genuine warmth

I am an integrative-relational psychotherapist registered with both the BACP and the UKCP. Before entering private practice, I worked in NHS settings, an experience that shaped my belief that good therapy should be grounded, responsive and genuinely tailored to the person in front of me.

My approach is truly integrative. I draw from psychodynamic, humanistic, existential, relational and systemic traditions, adapting to what each person actually needs rather than fitting people to a pre-set model. You are different from anyone else I have worked with, and the therapy should reflect that.

What matters most to me is the quality of the therapeutic relationship. A calm, thoughtful space where you feel genuinely heard and not judged is the foundation from which real change becomes possible. I work with adults and older adults, and also with therapists in training.

"Feeling understood, respected and taken seriously is often central to making sense of difficult experiences and creating meaningful change."

Jack Brenman

How I work

Truly integrative, genuinely relational

Integrative therapy means drawing from the best of multiple traditions rather than being bound by one. I adapt my approach to what each person brings, across four broad orientations.

01

Depth and unconscious

Working with early experience, patterns beneath the surface, and the existential dimensions of a human life. Psychodynamic, existential and phenomenological in orientation.

02

Relational and humanistic

The therapeutic relationship itself as a vehicle for change. Person-centred, interpersonal and emotionally focused approaches that begin with how we are with each other.

03

Systems and parts

Understanding you within your wider contexts: relationships, family, culture and the internal parts that shape how you think, feel and act. IFS and systemic frameworks.

04

Focused and time-aware

Therapy does not always need to be open-ended. Where that is what suits you, I offer short-term, brief and time-limited work with clear goals and a defined ending.
